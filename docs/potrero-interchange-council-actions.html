Potrero Interchange Council Actions

Council role

Major infrastructure requires repeated public approvals

The Potrero Interchange did not advance through a single City Council decision.

The project required a sequence of public actions involving funding agreements, cooperative agreements, consultant contracts, maintenance responsibilities, and other implementation decisions.

These actions document Beaumont's continuing municipal role even as Phase II became a regional construction effort involving RCTC, WRCOG, Caltrans, and other transportation partners.

March 4, 2025

Cooperative agreement for Phase II construction

On March 4, 2025, the Beaumont City Council agenda included a cooperative agreement involving the Riverside County Transportation Commission, Western Riverside Council of Governments, and the City of Beaumont.

The agreement concerned construction of the Potrero Boulevard Interchange Phase II project.

The recommended action was to approve the cooperative agreement and authorize the City Manager to execute it on behalf of Beaumont.

TUMF reimbursement agreement amendment

The same March 4, 2025 Council agenda also included Amendment No. 2 to the Transportation Uniform Mitigation Fee Program agreement for the Potrero Boulevard Interchange.

The agenda item concerned the WRCOG reimbursement agreement supporting TUMF funding for the project.

The recommended action was to authorize the Mayor to execute Amendment No. 2.

April 15, 2025

Landscape maintenance agreement with Caltrans

On April 15, 2025, the City Council agenda included a landscape maintenance agreement between the State of California, acting through Caltrans, and the City of Beaumont.

The agreement concerned maintenance responsibilities associated with the Potrero Interchange.

The recommended action was to approve the agreement and authorize the Mayor to execute it on behalf of the City.

July 2025

State funding formally obligated

In July 2025, Beaumont announced that the California Transportation Commission had formally obligated $33.5 million in Trade Corridor Enhancement Program funding for Phase II.

The release of those funds allowed the project to move forward with construction activities.

While the funding obligation itself was a state action rather than a City Council vote, it represented a major implementation milestone made possible by the agreements and funding structure previously supported through City actions.

August 19, 2025

Mark Thomas professional-services agreement amendment

On August 19, 2025, the City Council agenda included a second amendment to the professional-services agreement with Mark Thomas for Potrero Interchange Phase II.

The recommended action increased the agreement by $655,045, producing a revised not-to-exceed amount of $3,787,706.

The item also called for a corresponding purchase-order change associated with the Phase II project.

Evidence standard

Agenda recommendations are not automatically final actions

Beaumont Intelligence distinguishes between what staff recommended and what the City Council ultimately approved.

An agenda packet can establish that an item was scheduled for Council consideration and document the recommended action.

Where final minutes, resolutions, executed agreements, or video have not yet been independently verified, this page describes the item as a recommended Council action rather than automatically treating it as a completed vote.

This approach helps preserve the difference between proposed, authorized, and executed project actions.

Intelligence assessment

Council actions reveal the machinery behind the project

Large infrastructure projects are often described publicly by their visible milestones: grants, groundbreaking ceremonies, and eventual openings.

The City Council record shows the less-visible implementation work required between those milestones.

Cooperative agreements, reimbursement agreements, consultant amendments, maintenance responsibilities, and funding approvals form the administrative framework that allows construction to proceed.

For the Potrero Interchange, the 2025 Council record documents the transition from a funded transportation plan into an actively coordinated construction project.
