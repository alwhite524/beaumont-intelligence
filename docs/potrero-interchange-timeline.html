Long-range infrastructure

A project developed over many years

The Potrero Boulevard Interchange is the result of a long-term transportation planning effort rather than a single construction decision.

Beaumont's growth south of State Route 60 created increasing demand for additional freeway access and stronger connections between developing areas of the City and the regional highway network.

The project ultimately developed as a phased interchange strategy: first establishing the Potrero Boulevard crossing over SR-60, followed by construction of the freeway ramps and expanded roadway system needed to create a functioning interchange.

2019

Phase I overcrossing completed

Phase I of the Potrero Boulevard Interchange project was completed in 2019.

This phase constructed the Potrero Boulevard overcrossing across State Route 60 and established the physical roadway connection between the Potrero Boulevard corridor and Western Knolls Avenue.

Phase I did not create the complete freeway interchange. Instead, it established the crossing that Phase II would later expand with freeway ramps and additional roadway improvements.

Phase II

From an overcrossing to a functioning interchange

Following completion of Phase I, project development continued toward Phase II.

Phase II represents the much larger step of transforming the Potrero Boulevard crossing into a functional connection with State Route 60.

The planned improvements include a six-ramp partial-cloverleaf interchange, widening Potrero Boulevard to six lanes across SR-60, realigning Western Knolls Avenue, and constructing the associated freeway-access improvements.

2023

$33.5 million state funding award announced

In 2023, the City announced a major funding breakthrough for Potrero Interchange Phase II.

The project received a $33.5 million award through California's Trade Corridor Enhancement Program.

The award provided a major share of the resources needed to move the interchange toward construction and reflected the project's importance to freight movement and regional transportation.

2024–2025

Preparing Phase II for construction

After the major state funding milestone, project work continued through the engineering, coordination, funding, and construction preparation needed for a complex state-highway interchange.

The project required coordination among the City of Beaumont, Riverside County Transportation Commission, Caltrans, and other transportation partners.

This period represents the transition from project development and funding toward construction delivery.

April 28, 2026

Phase II groundbreaking

On April 28, 2026, the Riverside County Transportation Commission and the City of Beaumont held a groundbreaking ceremony for Phase II of the State Route 60/Potrero Boulevard Interchange Project.
