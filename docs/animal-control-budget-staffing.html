Program resources

A small municipal field operation

Beaumont Animal Control operates with a relatively small authorized field staff while supporting a broad range of enforcement, public-safety, animal-welfare, and service responsibilities.

Available City budget records show an important staffing change during the program's recent history: authorized staffing declined from 3.0 full-time-equivalent positions in FY 2018 to 2.0 FTE beginning by FY 2019.

The available records reviewed for this Intelligence Center do not yet establish the specific reason for that reduction. Rather than infer a cause, the staffing change is preserved as a documented fact with the explanation identified as a research gap.

Capacity

Staffing numbers tell only part of the story

Authorized staffing is an important measure of program capacity, but it does not by itself describe the full Animal Control service model.

Beaumont supplements its municipal field operation through contracted services. Shelter infrastructure is provided externally, and licensing administration has also been supported through an outside provider.

As a result, the City's direct Animal Control staffing level should not be interpreted as representing every person or organization involved in delivering animal-related services to Beaumont residents.
