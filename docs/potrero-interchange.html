Latest documented milestone

Phase II construction is underway after the April 28, 2026 groundbreaking.

The Riverside County Transportation Commission and the City of Beaumont jointly held the groundbreaking ceremony marking the start of construction for Phase II of the State Route 60 / Potrero Boulevard Interchange Project.

The project is intended to improve traffic flow, safety, freeway access, freight movement, emergency response, and regional connectivity as Beaumont and the San Gorgonio Pass continue to grow.

Project history

Phase I established the freeway crossing

The Potrero Boulevard Interchange project has been developed in multiple phases.

Phase I was completed in 2019 and constructed the Potrero Boulevard overcrossing across State Route 60.

That overpass created the physical connection between the Potrero Boulevard corridor and Western Knolls Avenue across the freeway.

Phase II builds on that earlier work by adding the freeway ramps and roadway improvements needed to create the functional interchange.

Funding milestone

Major state transportation funding helped unlock Phase II

One of the project's most significant funding milestones occurred in 2023, when Beaumont announced a $33.5 million award through California's Trade Corridor Enhancement Program.

The funding was intended to support engineering, construction, and other expenses associated with Phase II.

The project also relies on a combination of City, regional, and state transportation funding.

Intelligence assessment

A long-planned project has moved into delivery

The Potrero Interchange is no longer primarily a planning or funding effort.

With Phase I already completed, major Phase II funding secured, and the April 2026 groundbreaking completed, the project has transitioned into active construction.

That transition is significant because the interchange has long been tied to Beaumont's growth strategy, freeway access, industrial development, freight movement, and regional circulation.

The next intelligence priority is therefore tracking construction, funding execution, schedule changes, traffic impacts, and eventual completion against the commitments documented in the public record.
