Potrero Interchange Project Design

The Phase II interchange configuration, roadway widening, ramp system, local street realignment, and relationship between Phase I and the current construction project.

Phase II scope Six-ramp partial cloverleaf interchange, six-lane Potrero Boulevard crossing, and Western Knolls Avenue realignment.

Design overview

Turning an overcrossing into a full freeway interchange

Phase II of the State Route 60/Potrero Boulevard Interchange Project builds on the overcrossing completed during Phase I.

The current project adds the freeway-access system needed to transform the Potrero Boulevard crossing into a functioning interchange.

The principal improvements include a six-ramp partial-cloverleaf interchange, widening Potrero Boulevard to six lanes, and realignment of Western Knolls Avenue.

Interchange type

Six-ramp partial cloverleaf

Six freeway ramps

Phase II includes six ramps connecting Potrero Boulevard with State Route 60.

These ramps create the direct freeway-access function that was not included in the Phase I overcrossing project.

Partial-cloverleaf configuration

The project uses a partial-cloverleaf interchange design rather than a conventional four-ramp diamond configuration.

The design is intended to manage turning movements and freeway access while fitting the local roadway network and surrounding development pattern.

Six lanes across State Route 60

Potrero Boulevard is being widened to six lanes as part of the Phase II interchange improvements.

The wider roadway is intended to accommodate traffic moving between the freeway ramps, nearby development, and the local street network.

The six-lane configuration also builds directly on the Phase I overcrossing completed in 2019.

Western Knolls Avenue

Local roadway realignment

Phase II also includes realignment of Western Knolls Avenue.

This local-street work is necessary to integrate the freeway ramps and interchange geometry with the surrounding roadway system.

The realignment is intended to improve local connectivity while supporting movement to and from the new interchange.

Phase relationship

Phase I and Phase II serve different purposes

Phase I

  • Completed in 2019
  • Constructed the Potrero Boulevard overcrossing
  • Created a six-lane crossing over SR-60
  • Established the physical east-west roadway connection

Phase II

  • Adds six freeway ramps
  • Creates the functional freeway interchange
  • Widens and completes roadway connections
  • Realigns Western Knolls Avenue
  • Integrates Potrero Boulevard with SR-60 access
Key distinction

Phase I created the crossing. Phase II creates the freeway-access system.

Transportation function

Direct freeway access for a growing corridor

The project is intended to provide improved access to SR-60 from the Potrero Boulevard corridor.

This access supports residential and economic growth in Beaumont and the wider San Gorgonio Pass while providing another connection between local streets and the regional highway network.

The interchange is also intended to improve freight movement and reduce congestion associated with longer or less-direct routes to existing freeway access points.

Design objectives

What the project is intended to accomplish

1

Regional Mobility

Improve connections between Beaumont's local roadway system and State Route 60.

2

Freeway Access

Provide direct on-ramp and off-ramp access through the Potrero Boulevard corridor.

3

Traffic Flow

Improve circulation by adding roadway capacity and distributing trips through an additional freeway connection.

4

Economic Growth

Support commercial, industrial, employment, and residential development in the growing San Gorgonio Pass.

5

Freight Movement

Improve access for goods movement while reducing unnecessary circulation through local streets.

Project location

SR-60 between Jack Rabbit Trail and the SR-60/I-10 interchange

RCTC identifies the project location along State Route 60 between Jack Rabbit Trail and the SR-60/I-10 interchange.

The Potrero Boulevard interchange therefore sits within a strategically important section of the regional freeway system serving Beaumont and the San Gorgonio Pass.

Its position west of the SR-60/I-10 interchange provides an additional freeway connection for development areas south of SR-60.

Construction period

Design is now moving into physical delivery

RCTC currently identifies the Phase II construction period as 2026 through 2028.

The April 28, 2026 groundbreaking marked the formal beginning of the construction phase, with construction activity expected to continue through the planned 2028 opening.

As work progresses, the design documented on this page will transition from planned improvements into completed physical infrastructure.

Intelligence assessment

The design completes a two-phase transportation strategy

The Phase II design makes more sense when viewed together with the Phase I overcrossing.

Phase I established the physical crossing over SR-60. Phase II adds the ramps, roadway connections, and local realignment necessary to convert that crossing into a functioning regional interchange.

The resulting design is intended to serve more than immediate traffic demand. It is infrastructure planned to support long-term mobility, economic growth, freight movement, and development within Beaumont and the San Gorgonio Pass.
