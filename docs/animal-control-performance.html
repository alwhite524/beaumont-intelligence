Performance record

Animal Control workload can be measured, but the public record does not yet provide every measure needed for a complete performance assessment.

Available City records document service-call volume, staffing, budget information, contracts, and operational responsibilities. Those records establish a foundation for evaluating the service.

Beaumont Intelligence separates documented workload from broader conclusions about effectiveness. A high number of calls, for example, establishes workload but does not by itself establish whether response times, outcomes, enforcement, or sheltering performance improved.

Documented workload

More than 2,000 calls for service annually

Available City reporting documents 2,428 Animal Control calls for service during FY 2024–25.

That equals an average of approximately 202 calls per month, although actual demand may vary significantly by season, incident type, and location.

Calls for service provide one useful measure of workload. They do not, however, show how many incidents required field response, how long those responses took, or what outcome resulted.

Staffing context

Workload should be evaluated alongside available personnel

Available budget records identify current Animal Control field staffing at 2.0 full-time-equivalent positions.

Historical records also show staffing changes over time. That matters because service-call totals alone do not show the workload being carried by each available employee.

A useful long-term performance record should therefore track calls, staffing, service hours, response times, enforcement activity, and outcomes together.

What can be measured

A broader performance framework

1

Calls for Service

Total requests received, incident types, geographic distribution, and changes in demand over time.

2

Response Time

Time between a request for service and field response, particularly for urgent or public-safety-related incidents.

3

Field Outcomes

Warnings, citations, animals returned to owners, impounds, investigations, and other case outcomes.

4

Sheltering Outcomes

Animals transferred to sheltering providers, reclaimed by owners, adopted, transferred, or otherwise resolved.

5

Cost of Service

Personnel, vehicles, sheltering agreements, supplies, equipment, and other costs associated with providing Animal Control services.

Accountability

Workload is not the same as performance

What the record can establish

  • Reported calls for service.
  • Authorized and available staffing.
  • Budget allocations and expenditures where reported.
  • Sheltering and service agreements.
  • Municipal responsibilities and enforcement authority.

What still needs stronger data

  • Response times by priority.
  • Disposition of individual calls.
  • Animal return-to-owner rates.
  • Shelter outcomes for Beaumont animals.
  • Citation and enforcement trends.
  • Geographic and seasonal demand patterns.
Questions to track

What residents should be able to understand

1

How quickly does the City respond?

Response-time reporting would help distinguish emergency, priority, and routine service performance.

2

What happens after a call?

Outcome data would show whether calls result in education, enforcement, impound, owner contact, or another resolution.

3

Where do Beaumont animals go?

Shelter-specific outcome data would make the contracted sheltering model easier for the public to evaluate.

4

Is staffing keeping pace?

Comparing staffing and workload over multiple years can show whether service capacity is keeping pace with community demand.

5

What does the service cost?

Connecting operational outcomes to budget and contract costs creates a more meaningful measure of public value.

Evidence standard

Performance claims require performance evidence

Beaumont Intelligence does not treat activity as proof of effectiveness.

Call volume can demonstrate demand. Staffing can demonstrate available capacity. Budgets can demonstrate public spending. None of those measures, standing alone, establishes the quality or effectiveness of the service.

As additional records become available, this page can evolve from a workload profile into a longitudinal performance dashboard that compares demand, resources, response, outcomes, and cost over time.
