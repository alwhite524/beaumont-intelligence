Animal Control History

Historical overview

From municipal service to regional provider

Beaumont Animal Control has evolved through several distinct operational periods.

The available public record shows that Beaumont maintained its own municipal Animal Control function while also developing contractual relationships that extended services beyond the City's boundaries.

The most clearly documented regional relationship involved the City of Calimesa. Official records establish that Beaumont was providing Animal Control services to Calimesa beginning in 2010, with the relationship continuing through June 30, 2023.

Timeline

Key documented milestones

2010

Calimesa Service Begins

Later official City records identify July 1, 2010 as the beginning of Beaumont's Animal Control service relationship with the City of Calimesa.

2018

Three Authorized FTE

Available budget records identify 3.0 Animal Control full-time-equivalent positions in FY 2018.

2019

Staffing at Two FTE

By FY 2019, available budget records show authorized Animal Control staffing at 2.0 FTE. The reason for this reduction remains an identified research gap.

2021

Regional Agreements Continue

Beaumont approved a new Calimesa Animal Control agreement covering July 1, 2021 through June 30, 2023.

2021

Shelter Agreement Renewed

Beaumont approved a multi-year shelter services agreement with the Ramona Humane Society in September 2021.

2023

Calimesa Service Ends

Beaumont's contracted Animal Control services for Calimesa concluded on June 30, 2023.

2024

Sheltering Model Changes

Changes involving the shelter provider and Beaumont's agreement altered certain intake services, including owner-surrender services.

Today

Beaumont-Focused Model

The current program is more focused on Animal Control services within Beaumont while continuing to rely on contracted sheltering and licensing support.

Regional service

The Calimesa relationship

Documented beginning

Later official records identify July 1, 2010 as the effective beginning of Beaumont's Animal Control services for Calimesa.

The original 2010 agreement has not been located in the digital records reviewed for this Intelligence Center.

2021 agreement

On June 1, 2021, the Beaumont City Council considered a renewed Animal Control services agreement with Calimesa.

The agreement covered the period from July 1, 2021 through June 30, 2023 and continued an already established regional service relationship.

Regional partnerships

Morongo service relationship

Beaumont's regional Animal Control role was not limited to Calimesa.

Available City records also document Animal Control service relationships involving the Morongo Band of Mission Indians.

These agreements demonstrate that Beaumont historically operated an Animal Control program with service responsibilities extending beyond municipal boundaries.

The available digital record for the Morongo relationship is less complete than the documentation available for the later Calimesa agreements, and earlier historical details remain an area for additional research.

Sheltering history

A long-term contracted shelter model

Throughout the documented regional service period, Beaumont relied on outside shelter infrastructure rather than operating its own full-service municipal animal shelter.

For many years, the Ramona Humane Society provided contracted sheltering services supporting Beaumont Animal Control.

In September 2021, the City approved a new multi-year shelter services agreement with the organization.

In 2024, the organization transitioned to the name Haven Pet Center, and Beaumont subsequently amended its shelter services arrangement as operating conditions and available intake services changed.

Operational transition

From regional service back to Beaumont

Regional model

  • Beaumont served its own residents.
  • Field personnel also supported outside jurisdictions.
  • Regional contracts provided cost-recovery revenue.
  • Sheltering supported the broader service structure.

Beaumont-focused model

  • Calimesa services concluded in 2023.
  • Regional service obligations decreased.
  • Associated Calimesa contract revenue ended.
  • The program became more focused on Beaumont operations.
Major transition

The end of the Calimesa agreement represents one of the most significant documented changes in Beaumont Animal Control's recent operational history.

Evidence limits

What the historical record does not yet establish

Digital records become less complete as the investigation moves farther back in time.

The original 2010 Beaumont-Calimesa agreement has not been located in the digital records reviewed to date. The 2010 start date is instead established through later official City records referring to the existing relationship.

The research also has not yet identified documentation explaining why authorized Animal Control staffing decreased from 3.0 FTE in FY 2018 to 2.0 FTE beginning by FY 2019.

These limitations are preserved as explicit research gaps rather than filled with assumptions.

Intelligence assessment

A program shaped by regional service and contraction

Beaumont Animal Control's operational history shows a relatively small municipal program that expanded its role through regional service agreements.

The Calimesa and Morongo relationships demonstrate that Beaumont developed capacity not only to serve its own residents but also to provide Animal Control services under contract.

The conclusion of the Calimesa agreement in 2023 marked a major transition. It reduced regional obligations and associated contract revenue while shifting the program toward a more Beaumont-focused service model.

At the same time, Beaumont continued its longstanding approach of combining municipal field operations with contracted sheltering and administrative support.
