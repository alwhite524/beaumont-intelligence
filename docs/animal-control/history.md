# Animal Control History

## Overview

The history of Beaumont Animal Control reflects the evolution of a municipal public safety service that, at various times, also provided animal control services beyond Beaumont through regional agreements.

Available public records document partnerships with neighboring jurisdictions, contracted shelter providers, and outside service organizations that helped shape the program's modern operating model.

Today, Animal Control operates within the Beaumont Police Department and primarily serves the City of Beaumont.

---

## Early History

Publicly available digital records documenting the earliest years of Beaumont Animal Control are limited.

Because reliable digital documentation becomes less complete further back in time, this Intelligence Center does not assign a specific founding date to the program without supporting evidence.

By 2010, however, Beaumont had developed sufficient Animal Control capacity to provide contracted services outside the City.

---

## Regional Expansion

### City of Calimesa

Beginning in 2010, Beaumont provided Animal Control services to the City of Calimesa through an interagency agreement.

The arrangement expanded Beaumont's Animal Control responsibilities beyond its municipal boundaries.

Over the following years, Beaumont Animal Control provided services to both communities under contractual arrangements that addressed service responsibilities and cost recovery.

The partnership continued for approximately thirteen years.

---

### Morongo Band of Mission Indians

Beaumont also provided Animal Control services through agreements involving the Morongo Band of Mission Indians.

These agreements further demonstrate the regional role Beaumont Animal Control played during portions of its operational history.

The availability of historical documentation varies by year, and additional records may expand this portion of the history in the future.

---

## Shelter Partnership

Beaumont has historically relied on contracted sheltering rather than operating its own municipal animal shelter.

For many years, sheltering services were associated with the Ramona Humane Society.

The shelter relationship provided services connected with animals handled or impounded through Beaumont Animal Control.

This contract-based model allowed the City to maintain Animal Control field operations without assuming the full operational responsibility of running a municipal shelter.

---

## 2021 Agreements

The year 2021 represents an important period in the documented history of the program.

### Calimesa Agreement

On June 1, 2021, the Beaumont City Council considered and approved an Animal Control services agreement involving the City of Calimesa.

The agreement continued the regional service relationship and addressed the financial and operational terms governing those services.

### Shelter Agreement

Later in 2021, the City approved a new shelter services agreement with the Ramona Humane Society.

The agreement continued Beaumont's reliance on a contracted shelter provider rather than a City-operated facility.

---

## End of Calimesa Services

Beaumont subsequently determined that the regional Animal Control arrangement with Calimesa would not continue indefinitely.

The City provided notice that services would end when the agreement expired.

Animal Control services to Calimesa concluded in 2023.

This represented a significant change in the program's regional role and returned the primary operational focus to Beaumont.

---

## Transition to Haven Pet Center

Another significant change occurred in 2024 when the Ramona Humane Society transitioned to the name Haven Pet Center.

The shelter provider was also undergoing changes to its operating model.

Those changes affected the services available under Beaumont's sheltering agreement.

---

## 2024 Shelter Changes

In 2024, Beaumont amended its sheltering arrangement in response to changes involving the shelter provider.

Among the significant operational changes were the removal of certain intake services, including owner-surrender services.

The change illustrates how Beaumont's Animal Control program depends not only on municipal policy but also on the capacity and operating model of its contracted service partners.

---

## Modern Program

Today, Beaumont Animal Control operates within the Beaumont Police Department.

The modern program focuses on functions including:

- Animal-related public safety response
- Municipal and state law enforcement
- Animal investigations
- Stray and loose animal response
- Licensing enforcement
- Coordination with contracted shelter services
- Public education and assistance

The program's current structure combines direct municipal field operations with contracted support services.

## Research Limitations

The historical record is not equally complete for every period.

In particular, older operational records may not be available digitally.

Where documentation is incomplete, this Intelligence Center avoids filling historical gaps with assumptions.

Additional official records may be incorporated as they are discovered.

---

## Intelligence Assessment

The documented history shows that Beaumont Animal Control evolved through three broad stages:

1. Development as a municipal Animal Control service.
2. Expansion into a regional service provider through interagency agreements.
3. Return toward a Beaumont-focused operating model supported by contracted sheltering and administrative services.

The precise origins of the program require additional historical research, but the available record provides a strong foundation for understanding its modern development.
