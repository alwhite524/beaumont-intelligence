\# Animal Control Intelligence Center



\## Purpose



The Animal Control Intelligence Center organizes official records, contracts, ordinances, budgets, council actions, service data, and public information related to animal control services in Beaumont, California.



Its purpose is to help residents understand:



\- Who provides animal control services

\- How those services are funded

\- What contracts and policies govern operations

\- How service levels and outcomes have changed over time

\- What decisions the City Council has made

\- What questions remain unanswered



\## Mission



Provide a clear, evidence-based, and source-linked record of Beaumont animal control policy, funding, operations, and public accountability.



\## Research Principles



\- Use official records whenever available

\- Link every significant claim to a source

\- Separate verified facts from analysis

\- Preserve historical context

\- Identify missing information clearly

\- Update the Intelligence Center when new records become available



\## Project Structure



\- `briefings` — published summaries and issue briefings

\- `data` — structured statistics, budgets, and datasets

\- `documents` — indexed official records and metadata

\- `evidence` — supporting citations and source analysis

\- `images` — approved images, diagrams, and graphics

\- `media` — meeting videos and media references

\- `research` — working notes, statutes, ordinances, and background research

\- `timeline` — chronological record of contracts, policy changes, and council actions



\## Initial Research Questions



1\. Who currently provides animal control services for Beaumont?

2\. What services are required under the current agreement?

3\. How much does Beaumont pay annually?

4\. What response-time and performance requirements apply?

5\. How many service calls, impounds, adoptions, returns, and euthanasia events are reported?

6\. What policies govern stray and feral cats?

7\. What complaints or controversies have appeared before the City Council?

8\. What ordinances and state laws govern local animal control?

9\. What alternatives have been considered?

10\. What information is still missing and may require a public records request?



\## Current Status



Foundation established.
