# Beaumont Municipal Code — Animal Regulations

## Overview

The Beaumont Municipal Code establishes the City's local legal framework for animal ownership, regulation, licensing, enforcement, and Animal Control authority.

The primary local animal regulations are contained in **Title 6 — Animals**.

Animal Control also operates under applicable California law. This page is therefore intended as a plain-language research guide to Beaumont's local regulatory framework, not as a replacement for the current Municipal Code or state law.

---

## Title 6 — Animals

Title 6 contains Beaumont's principal municipal regulations concerning animals.

Major subject areas include:

- General animal regulations
- Dogs and cats
- Animal nuisances
- Animal licensing
- Animal Control enforcement authority
- Animal ownership responsibilities

---

## Chapter 6.02 — Regulations Generally

Chapter 6.02 establishes many of the City's general animal regulations.

Topics addressed include:

- Definitions
- Responsibilities of animal owners
- Spay and neuter requirements
- Microchipping requirements
- Sale or transfer of animals
- Animals left in vehicles
- Dangerous or restricted animals
- Animal abandonment
- Animal Services Officer authority
- Enforcement and cost recovery

These provisions provide an important portion of the legal foundation for Animal Control field operations.

---

## Spay and Neuter Requirements

The Municipal Code contains requirements relating to the alteration of dogs and cats.

These provisions support:

- Population control
- Responsible animal ownership
- Reduction of unwanted animals

Specific requirements and exemptions should always be verified against the current Municipal Code before relying on them for legal or compliance purposes.

---

## Microchipping

The Municipal Code also contains microchipping requirements applicable to dogs and cats.

Microchips can assist Animal Control and shelter personnel with:

- Identifying owned animals
- Contacting owners
- Returning lost animals
- Establishing ownership information

---

## Animal Abandonment

Abandonment of animals is prohibited.

Animal abandonment may also implicate California animal cruelty and welfare laws in addition to local municipal requirements.

---

## Animal Services Officer Authority

Title 6 provides authority for Animal Services Officers to perform duties associated with enforcement of the City's animal regulations.

Depending on the circumstances and applicable law, enforcement activity may include:

- Investigation
- Citation
- Impoundment
- Compliance action
- Cost recovery
- Coordination with law enforcement

---

## Chapter 6.04 — Dogs and Cats

Chapter 6.04 addresses regulations specifically involving dogs and cats.

Major subjects include:

- Dogs running at large
- Impoundment
- Roaming animals
- Cat-related regulations
- Animal nuisances
- Barking dogs
- Guard dog requirements

These provisions are particularly relevant to routine Animal Control calls for service.

---

## Dogs Running at Large

Dogs are subject to restrictions against running at large.

Animal Control may take enforcement action when dogs are found loose in violation of applicable requirements.

Depending on the circumstances, enforcement may include:

- Contact with the owner
- Citation
- Impoundment
- Other authorized enforcement action

---

## Animal Nuisances

The Municipal Code regulates animal-related nuisances.

Nuisance conditions may involve:

- Excessive or persistent barking
- Animals creating disturbances
- Repeated violations involving animal behavior
- Conditions affecting neighboring properties

The appropriate enforcement response depends on the specific facts and applicable Code provisions.

---

## Chapter 6.05 — Disposal of Dog and Horse Feces

Chapter 6.05 addresses the proper removal and disposal of certain animal waste.

These requirements support:

- Public sanitation
- Neighborhood cleanliness
- Public health
- Responsible animal ownership

---

## Chapter 6.06 — Animal Licensing

Chapter 6.06 establishes the City's animal licensing requirements.

Subjects include:

- Dog licenses
- License periods
- License tags
- Replacement tags
- Display requirements
- Other licensing procedures

Licensing administration may be supported by an outside contractor, but enforcement remains part of the City's Animal Control framework.

---

## Relationship to Zoning Regulations

Animal regulations are not limited to Title 6.

**Title 17 — Zoning** may also affect the keeping of animals on private property.

Depending on the zoning designation and property characteristics, regulations may address:

- Number of animals
- Horses
- Livestock
- Poultry
- Agricultural animals
- Property requirements associated with animal keeping

Some cases may therefore involve coordination between Animal Control and other City functions such as Code Enforcement or Planning.

---

## Relationship to California Law

Beaumont's Municipal Code operates alongside California law.

State statutes may govern or supplement local requirements involving matters such as:

- Rabies control
- Animal bites
- Quarantine
- Animal cruelty
- Dangerous animals
- Veterinary requirements
- Impoundment
- Animal welfare

Local regulations cannot be evaluated in isolation when state law also applies.

---

## Enforcement Framework

Animal Control enforcement may involve several levels of response.

Depending on the violation, these can include:

1. Education and voluntary compliance
2. Warning or notice
3. Citation
4. Impoundment
5. Administrative proceedings
6. Coordination with law enforcement
7. Referral for additional legal action when appropriate

The specific response depends on the circumstances and legal authority applicable to the incident.

## Research Use

The Municipal Code is one of the highest-authority sources used by the Animal Control Intelligence Center.

When researching a specific issue, the preferred process is:

1. Identify the applicable Municipal Code provision.
2. Verify that the provision is current.
3. Determine whether California law also applies.
4. Review related City policies or administrative procedures.
5. Review relevant City Council actions when historical context is necessary.

## Intelligence Assessment

Title 6 provides the primary local legal framework for Beaumont Animal Control.

However, the actual operation of the program is shaped by several layers of authority and administration:

- Beaumont Municipal Code
- California law
- City Council actions
- Department procedures
- Service agreements

Understanding Beaumont Animal Control therefore requires examining both the legal framework and the operational system used to implement it.
