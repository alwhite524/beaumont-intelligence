# Animal Control Contracts

## Overview

Beaumont Animal Control has relied on contracts and interagency agreements to provide several components of its service model.

These agreements have historically supported:

- Regional Animal Control services
- Animal sheltering
- Licensing administration
- Specialized operational services

This page documents the major contractual relationships identified through the Animal Control Intelligence Center research.

---

## City of Calimesa

### Regional Animal Control Services

Beaumont historically provided Animal Control services to the City of Calimesa through an interagency agreement.

Later official records identify **July 1, 2010** as the beginning of the Beaumont-Calimesa Animal Control service relationship.

The agreement allowed Beaumont Animal Control personnel to provide services outside Beaumont while Calimesa reimbursed Beaumont according to the applicable contractual terms.

---

### June 1, 2021 Agreement

On June 1, 2021, the Beaumont City Council approved an agreement continuing Animal Control services for the City of Calimesa.

The agreement covered an initial term from:

**July 1, 2021 through June 30, 2023**

The 2021 agreement represented a continuation and restructuring of an existing regional service relationship rather than the beginning of Animal Control services between the two cities.

The agreement addressed matters including:

- Animal Control field services
- Service responsibilities
- Compensation and cost recovery
- Administrative responsibilities
- Contract duration

---

### End of Calimesa Services

In 2022, Beaumont notified Calimesa that the Animal Control agreement would not be renewed beyond its expiration.

Beaumont's contracted Animal Control services to Calimesa therefore concluded:

**June 30, 2023**

Calimesa subsequently transitioned its Animal Control services to Riverside County.

The available evidence establishes when the agreement ended but does not establish a definitive internal Beaumont rationale for the decision not to renew.

---

## Morongo Band of Mission Indians

Beaumont also entered into agreements involving the Morongo Band of Mission Indians for Animal Control field services.

Available records demonstrate that Beaumont had developed sufficient personnel, equipment, and operational capability to provide Animal Control services beyond the City's municipal boundaries.

Documented agreements include service arrangements during the 2010s and subsequent renewal activity.

These agreements form an important part of Beaumont Animal Control's history as a regional service provider.

---

## Animal Sheltering

### Ramona Humane Society

For many years, Beaumont relied on the Ramona Humane Society for contracted animal sheltering services.

The relationship allowed Beaumont to maintain Animal Control field operations without operating its own full-service municipal animal shelter.

Contracted sheltering functions included services associated with:

- Housing impounded animals
- Animal care
- Owner redemption
- Adoption
- Other shelter-related services

---

### 2021 Shelter Agreement

In September 2021, the Beaumont City Council approved a new multi-year shelter services agreement with the Ramona Humane Society.

The agreement continued the City's contract-based sheltering model.

At that time, the shelter relationship also supported the broader regional Animal Control structure involving Beaumont and Calimesa.

---

## Haven Pet Center

Effective in 2024, the Ramona Humane Society began operating as Haven Pet Center.

The organizational transition was accompanied by changes to the shelter provider's operating model and available services.

As a result, Beaumont's sheltering agreement was amended.

---

### 2024 Shelter Amendment

The 2024 amendment changed the scope of services provided under the sheltering arrangement.

Significant changes included removal of:

- Owner-surrender services
- Certain feral or stray cat intake services

Sheltering services associated with animals handled through Beaumont Animal Control continued under the revised arrangement.

The amendment demonstrates the relationship between contracted shelter capacity and the services Beaumont can make available through its Animal Control program.

---

## Licensing Administration

Beaumont has also used an outside service provider for animal licensing administration.

### PetData

PetData supports licensing functions such as:

- License issuance
- License renewals
- Replacement tags
- Administrative processing
- Online licensing services

Animal Control retains responsibility for enforcement of applicable licensing requirements.

## Research Limitations

Some historical agreements predate the City's currently accessible digital meeting records.

For those periods, later official records may establish the existence or effective date of an agreement even when the original agenda packet or executed agreement has not yet been located digitally.

The Intelligence Center distinguishes those historical references from agreements for which complete digital documentation is available.

## Intelligence Assessment

Contracts have played a central role in the development of Beaumont Animal Control.

Historically, agreements allowed Beaumont to extend Animal Control services beyond city boundaries.

At the same time, external contracts allowed the City to obtain sheltering and administrative capabilities without building every component of the Animal Control system internally.

The modern program therefore reflects a hybrid model:

- Municipal field operations and enforcement
- Contracted sheltering
- Contracted administrative support
- Historical regional service agreements
