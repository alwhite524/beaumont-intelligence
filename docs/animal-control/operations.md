# Animal Control Operations

## Overview

Animal Control services in Beaumont are administered through the Beaumont Police Department.

The program is responsible for enforcing animal-related laws, responding to animal-related calls for service, supporting public safety, coordinating sheltering services, and providing other animal control functions authorized by municipal and state law.

---

## Organization

Animal Control operates as part of the Beaumont Police Department.

This organizational placement reflects the program's public safety and enforcement responsibilities, including:

- Animal-related calls for service
- Enforcement of municipal animal regulations
- Dangerous or aggressive animal incidents
- Animal bite investigations
- Stray animal response
- Welfare and cruelty investigations
- Coordination with sheltering providers
- Licensing-related enforcement

---

## Service Area

The current primary service area is the City of Beaumont.

Historically, Beaumont also provided animal control services to outside jurisdictions through regional agreements, including the City of Calimesa and the Morongo Band of Mission Indians.

Those regional arrangements demonstrate that Beaumont's Animal Control program previously operated beyond city limits under contract.

---

## Core Services

### Calls for Service

Animal Control officers respond to a range of animal-related incidents reported by residents, law enforcement personnel, and other agencies.

Typical calls may include:

- Stray dogs
- Loose animals
- Aggressive animals
- Injured animals
- Animal bites
- Animal cruelty or neglect
- Deceased animals
- Barking or nuisance complaints
- Wildlife-related concerns

---

## Enforcement

Animal Control officers enforce applicable provisions of the Beaumont Municipal Code and relevant California law.

Enforcement may include:

- Citations
- Notices of violation
- Impoundment
- Dangerous animal proceedings
- Licensing enforcement
- Quarantine requirements
- Investigation of cruelty or neglect

---

## Investigations

Certain animal-related incidents require formal investigation.

Examples include:

- Animal bites
- Dangerous or vicious animal cases
- Suspected cruelty or neglect
- Repeat nuisance violations
- Cases involving public safety concerns

Animal Control may coordinate with other Police Department personnel when incidents involve broader criminal or public safety issues.

---

## Public Services

The program also provides services intended to assist residents and promote responsible animal ownership.

These may include:

- Guidance regarding lost or found animals
- Licensing information
- Shelter referrals
- Educational information
- Assistance related to animal-related municipal requirements

---

## Sheltering Model

Beaumont does not operate Animal Control and animal sheltering as the same function.

Animal Control is the municipal enforcement and response program.

Sheltering services are provided through contractual partnerships.

The current sheltering relationship is associated with Haven Pet Center.

This separation allows the City to maintain field enforcement and public safety responsibilities while using an external shelter provider for animal housing and related shelter services.

---

## Licensing

Animal licensing is an important component of the Animal Control system.

Licensing supports:

- Identification of owned animals
- Rabies compliance
- Recovery of lost animals
- Enforcement of municipal requirements
- Program revenue

The City has used an outside provider, PetData, to support licensing administration.

---

## Owner Surrenders

Owner surrender services are not currently part of the standard Animal Control service model.

Changes to the City's sheltering arrangement in 2024 removed owner surrender services from the contracted sheltering structure.

Residents seeking to surrender personally owned animals may therefore need to use alternative shelter or rescue resources.

---

## Emergency Response

Animal Control functions may intersect with emergency response when incidents involve immediate threats to public safety.

Examples include:

- Aggressive animals
- Serious animal bites
- Animals creating traffic hazards
- Animals involved in law enforcement incidents
- Situations involving injured or endangered animals

Because the program operates within the Police Department, Animal Control can coordinate directly with sworn personnel when necessary.

---

## Partnerships

Animal Control relies on multiple relationships to provide the full range of services.

These include:

- Beaumont Police Department
- Haven Pet Center
- PetData
- Veterinary providers
- Regional agencies
- Other municipal departments

Historical partnerships have also included:

- City of Calimesa
- Morongo Band of Mission Indians
- Ramona Humane Society

## Intelligence Assessment

The available evidence shows that Beaumont operates a hybrid Animal Control model.

The City retains direct responsibility for field response, enforcement, and public safety functions while relying on external partners for sheltering and certain administrative services.

Historically, the program also served as a regional provider under contractual agreements with neighboring or affiliated jurisdictions.

The current structure appears more focused on Beaumont's internal public safety responsibilities, with sheltering and licensing functions supported through contracted service providers.
