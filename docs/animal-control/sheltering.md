# Animal Sheltering Services

## Overview

The City of Beaumont does not operate its own full-service municipal animal shelter.

Instead, Beaumont uses a contracted sheltering model in which Animal Control field operations remain a City responsibility while animal housing and related shelter services are provided by an outside organization.

This distinction is important:

- **Animal Control** handles field response, enforcement, investigations, and animal-related public safety.
- **The contracted shelter provider** handles the care and housing of animals delivered through the Animal Control system.

---

## Operating Model

Beaumont's current Animal Control system separates several major functions.

| Function | Provider |
|---|---|
| Animal Control Administration | Beaumont Police Department |
| Field Response and Enforcement | Beaumont Animal Control |
| Animal Sheltering | Contracted shelter provider |
| Current Shelter Partner | Haven Pet Center |
| Licensing Administration | PetData |

This model allows Beaumont to maintain municipal Animal Control operations without operating its own full-service shelter facility.

---

## Historical Shelter Partnership

For many years, Beaumont relied on the **Ramona Humane Society** for contracted animal sheltering services.

The relationship supported Beaumont's Animal Control operations by providing services associated with animals impounded or otherwise transferred through the City's Animal Control program.

Historical shelter functions included:

- Animal housing
- Animal care
- Owner redemption
- Adoption services
- Veterinary coordination
- Other shelter-related services

The shelter partnership also supported Beaumont during the period when the City provided contracted Animal Control services to Calimesa.

---

## 2021 Shelter Agreement

In September 2021, the Beaumont City Council approved a new multi-year shelter services agreement with the Ramona Humane Society.

The agreement continued Beaumont's established contract-based sheltering model.

Rather than constructing and staffing a municipal shelter, the City continued using an established regional shelter provider while maintaining Animal Control field operations through the Beaumont Police Department.

---

## Transition to Haven Pet Center

In 2024, the Ramona Humane Society transitioned to the name **Haven Pet Center**.

The transition occurred alongside changes in the organization's operating model and shelter capacity.

City records associated with the subsequent contract amendment identified operational pressures affecting the sheltering system.

These included issues involving:

- Animal population
- Shelter capacity
- Staffing
- Veterinary resources
- Available intake services

These conditions affected the scope of services that could continue under Beaumont's shelter agreement.

---

## 2024 Shelter Agreement Amendment

Beaumont subsequently amended its shelter services agreement.

The amendment changed certain intake services while maintaining the core sheltering relationship for animals handled through Beaumont Animal Control.

Significant changes included removal of:

- Owner-surrender services
- Certain feral or stray cat intake services

The amendment illustrates an important feature of Beaumont's Animal Control model:

Changes involving the contracted shelter provider can directly affect which animal services are available through the City.

---

## Owner Surrenders

Beginning in February 2024, owner-surrendered animals were no longer accepted as part of Beaumont's standard contracted Animal Control sheltering model.

This means residents seeking to voluntarily surrender personally owned animals may need to use alternative resources, including:

- Rescue organizations
- Rehoming services
- Other shelter providers
- Community animal welfare resources

This policy should not be confused with Animal Control's authority to respond to animals encountered through enforcement or public safety activities.

---

## Field Operations vs. Shelter Operations

Animal Control and animal sheltering perform related but distinct functions.

### Animal Control

Responsibilities may include:

- Responding to calls for service
- Enforcement
- Investigations
- Animal impoundment
- Animal transportation
- Public safety response
- Licensing enforcement

### Shelter Provider

Responsibilities may include:

- Housing animals
- Daily animal care
- Medical evaluation
- Owner redemption
- Adoption processing
- Other contracted shelter functions

Keeping these responsibilities separate helps explain why a change in shelter intake policy does not necessarily represent a reduction in Animal Control field enforcement.

---

## Operational Dependency

The contracted shelter model provides significant operational capability without requiring Beaumont to construct and operate its own shelter.

However, the model also creates dependency on:

- Contract terms
- Shelter capacity
- Shelter staffing
- Veterinary availability
- Provider operating policies
- Contract renewal and amendment decisions

The 2024 changes demonstrate how conditions at an external shelter provider can affect municipal service availability.

---

## Historical Significance

The shelter relationship has been a central component of Beaumont's Animal Control program for many years.

It allowed the City to:

- Maintain Animal Control field personnel
- Provide sheltering for impounded animals
- Support regional Animal Control agreements
- Avoid operating a standalone municipal shelter

As Beaumont's regional service relationships changed, the sheltering arrangement also evolved.

---

## Intelligence Assessment

Beaumont's Animal Control program is built around a deliberate separation between municipal field operations and contracted sheltering.

The City maintains direct responsibility for enforcement and animal-related public safety while relying on an outside organization for shelter infrastructure and animal care.

This arrangement reduces the need for Beaumont to operate its own shelter, but it also means changes in the contracted shelter provider's capacity or operating model can directly affect the services available to Beaumont residents.

The 2024 shelter amendment provides a clear example of that relationship.
