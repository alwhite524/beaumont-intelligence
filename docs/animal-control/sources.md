# Animal Control Source Register

## Overview

This Source Register catalogs the official records and other authoritative sources used to build the Beaumont Animal Control Intelligence Center.

The purpose of the register is to make the research:

- Traceable
- Verifiable
- Maintainable
- Reusable for future investigations

Where possible, conclusions in the Intelligence Center should be supported by original City records rather than secondary summaries.

---

## Source Standards

Primary sources used by this Intelligence Center include:

- City Council agenda packets
- Staff reports
- Executed agreements
- Resolutions and ordinances
- Adopted budgets
- Beaumont Municipal Code
- Official City and department publications
- Official agency records

When an original historical document has not been located digitally, later official records may be used to establish historical facts, provided the limitation is clearly identified.

**Source Type:** Historical agreement references contained in later City records

# Source Reliability

The Intelligence Center generally applies the following preference order when evaluating evidence:

1. Municipal Code and applicable law
2. Executed agreements
3. Ordinances and resolutions
4. Adopted City budgets
5. City Council staff reports and agenda records
6. Official departmental publications
7. Official City webpages
8. Other authoritative government records

Secondary reporting may be useful for identifying potential research leads but should not replace available primary government records.

---

# Research Principle

The absence of a record is not treated as proof that an event did or did not occur.

When historical documentation cannot be located, the Intelligence Center identifies the limitation rather than filling the gap with assumptions.

As additional official records are identified, this Source Register and the related Intelligence Center documents should be updated accordingly.
