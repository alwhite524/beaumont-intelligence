# Animal Control Budget and Staffing

## Overview

Beaumont Animal Control operates within the Beaumont Police Department and is supported through City funding, program revenues, and historically, revenue from regional service agreements.

Public budget documents provide useful information about staffing, operating priorities, revenues, contracts, vehicles, and equipment. However, not every Animal Control cost is reported as a standalone program expense.

---

## Staffing

Animal Control field services are performed by Animal Control personnel within the Beaumont Police Department.

Available City budget records indicate that the program has operated with:

- **3.0 Full-Time Equivalent positions in FY 2018**
- **2.0 Full-Time Equivalent positions beginning by FY 2019**
- **2.0 Full-Time Equivalent positions during the more recent budget periods reviewed**

This indicates that the authorized staffing level decreased from three positions to two before the period covered by much of the current Animal Control research.

---

## Staffing Research Gap

The available budget records establish that the staffing reduction occurred, but the research completed to date does not establish the specific reason for the reduction from 3.0 to 2.0 FTE.

Possible explanations should not be assumed without supporting records.

This remains an identified research gap.

## Program Funding

Animal Control has historically been supported through a combination of:

- General Fund resources
- Animal licensing revenue
- Service fees
- Citation-related revenue
- Cost recovery
- Regional contract reimbursements

The exact combination has changed as the program's service area and contractual relationships evolved.

Licensing supports several public purposes, including:

- Identification of owned animals
- Rabies compliance
- Recovery of lost animals
- Enforcement of ownership requirements

Revenue may include:

- Initial licenses
- License renewals
- Replacement tags
- Related administrative charges

Licensing administration is supported through the City's outside licensing provider.

## Regional Contract Revenue

For many years, Beaumont received revenue for providing Animal Control services outside the City.

The most significant documented regional relationship involved the City of Calimesa.

The Calimesa agreement provided a mechanism for Beaumont to recover costs associated with delivering contracted Animal Control services.

After Beaumont's services to Calimesa concluded in 2023, the associated cost-recovery revenue no longer appeared as an ongoing component of the regional service model.

---

## Morongo Services

Agreements involving the Morongo Band of Mission Indians also formed part of Beaumont's historical regional Animal Control operations.

These arrangements demonstrate that the program historically used service agreements to offset or recover costs associated with providing Animal Control services beyond Beaumont.

---

## Sheltering Costs

Because Beaumont does not operate its own full-service municipal animal shelter, sheltering represents an important contracted expense.

Historically, the City's sheltering provider was the Ramona Humane Society.

The current sheltering relationship is associated with Haven Pet Center.

Contracted shelter services reduce the need for Beaumont to directly fund:

- Shelter construction
- Kennel staffing
- Full-time shelter operations
- Shelter veterinary infrastructure
- Daily animal care operations

However, the City remains responsible for paying for contracted sheltering services according to applicable agreements.

---

## Budget and Service Evolution

The financial structure of Animal Control changed as Beaumont's regional role changed.

### Regional Model

During the period when Beaumont served outside jurisdictions:

- The City incurred additional service obligations.
- Contract reimbursement supported cost recovery.
- Resources served both Beaumont and contract partners.

### Beaumont-Focused Model

Following the conclusion of Calimesa services:

- Regional service obligations decreased.
- Calimesa-related cost recovery ended.
- The program became more focused on services within Beaumont.

This represents an important change in both the operational and financial structure of the program.

---

## Public Reporting Limitations

Public budget documents do not consistently provide every Animal Control expense as a separate line item.

Information that may not be independently reported includes:

- Cost per Animal Control call
- Cost per impounded animal
- Shelter cost per animal
- Detailed enforcement revenue
- Licensing compliance rates
- Complete administrative overhead
- Detailed historical staffing explanations

These limitations should be considered when evaluating program finances.

---

## Intelligence Assessment

The available evidence shows that Beaumont has maintained a relatively small municipal Animal Control field operation while using contractual relationships to provide services that would otherwise require additional City infrastructure.

The program's financial model has historically combined:

- Municipal funding
- Program-generated revenue
- Regional cost recovery
- Contracted sheltering
- Contracted administrative services

The end of the Calimesa relationship in 2023 represents an important financial and operational transition because it reduced both regional service obligations and associated contract revenue.

The documented reduction from 3.0 to 2.0 FTE is also significant, although the reason for that staffing change remains an unresolved research question.
